Trash Dinner

Description

The gut-twisting, bowel-disrupting, blood sugar-upsetting dinner of your dreams.

Ingredients

Kraft Macaroni and Cheese

  • Kraft Macaroni and Cheese box dinner
  • 1/4 cup 2% milk
  • 4 tbsp butter

Mini Crescent Dogs

  • One can of Pillsbury crescent rolls
  • One pack of cocktail weenies
  • Optional: Several slices of cheese

Steps (Mini Crescent Dogs)

  1. Preheat oven to 375Â°F.
  2. Prepare a plate to set your crescent roll pieces on.
  3. Prepare a baking sheet. There is no need to grease, but it also doesn't hurt anything if you do.
  4. Peel back label on can of crescent rolls and burst as indicated on packaging. Discard canister.
  5. Unroll and break apart into discrete triangles of bread goo.
  6. Cut each triangle into at least three other triangles. Figure out the best way to do this on a piece-to-piece basis, preferring long triangles wherever possible.
  7. Open your pack of weenies (sausage, hot dog, whatever you prefer) and drain it of gross preservative goo. Dry off weenies in a paper towel for easier handling. The crescent rolls will adhere more easily to less-slimey weenies.
  8. One after another, take a sliced triangle of crescent roll in your dominant hand, and a weenie in the other, and wrap the latter in the former. Like a pig in some sort of blanket.
  9. As you wrap your weenies, deposit them evenly across the baking sheet until there are no more.
  10. Place the baking sheet in the oven for 12-15 minutes. I happen to think they're perfect right at 12 minutes, but you might like a little more color to yours.

Steps (Kraft Macaroni and Cheese)

  1. Boil about six cups of water
  2. Once boiling, add in noodles. Stir occasionally to keep them broken up and from adhering to the bottom of the pot where they will burn.
  3. After 7 and 1/2 minutes, drain the pot of water.
  4. Tear open the included cheese-flavored powder packet and empty over noodles.
  5. Add 1/4 cup milk.
  6. Add 4 tbsp butter.
  7. Stir until it all comes together and looks like the picture on the front of the box.
